Why the i/ & m/?

You may be wondering why there are "i/" or "/m" before the food dropdown search results. Although not necessary to type, this is due to the way the databases are coded, using seperate databases for meals and ingredients respectively (since their result pages vary slightly). I am not a professional web developer and wanted to build this app to aid the gap in having easily accessible oxalate contents in complete meals, as well as a change history of research studies for individual ingredients. The code for this website is open-source and can be found on my github page.
